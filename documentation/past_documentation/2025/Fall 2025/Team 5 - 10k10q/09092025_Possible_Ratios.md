# <a name="_jfr6nzijn24"></a>Ratios

1. **Liquidity** (pay short term obligations) (within 1 year)
   1. **Current ratio** = Current assets / Current liabilities
      1. Above 1.0 the company can pay for its current liabilities 
      1. Above 3.0 this could mean that the company is not using its current assets as effective as possible (could invest into the business)
   1. **Return on Assets** = Net Income / Total assets 
      1. High means that the assets are being utilized very efficiently
1. **Profitability** (generating decent returns)
   1. **Gross profit margin** = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
      1. Goal is a high profit margin → profitability 
   1. **Net profit margin**
   1. **Operating profit margin**
1. **Efficiency** (how well does the company use the assets)
   1. **Asset Turnover** = Revenue / Average Total Assets
      1. This means for every dollar the company has in assets, the company generate $x amount of sales
      1. Depends on the industry!
1. **Market Ratio** (How the market values the company)
   1. **Earnings per share (EPS)** = Net Income / Shares Outstanding (gathered from YAHOO Finance)
      1. This basically means how much profit the company was able to make each outstanding share 
      1. The higher the better → long term growth (company is more profitable per share)
   1. **Price-to-earnings ratio (P/E)** = Share price /  EPS (gathered from YAHOO Finance)
      1. EPS = Earnings-per-share 
      1. This ratio explain what the investors are willing to pay for $1 of the company earnings 
      1. The best moment to invest and knowing at which value really depends on the industry 
         1. High → could mean a strong growth but at the same time that the company is overvalued (too high price at the time)
         1. Low → could mean that the company is undervalued but as well the company could be underperforming, which means a bad outlook
   1. **Debt-to-equity ratio** = Total Debt / shareholders equity 
      1. Indicates how much debt the company is using to finance the growth of the business 
         1. High → company is very dependent on debt to finance the business (more risk)
         1. Low → company is more relying on equity (less risk; but the company might not be using all its potential for growth)
   1. **Price-to-book (P/B)** = stock price / book-value per share 
      1. Book value per share = (total assets - total liabilities - preferred equity) / outstanding shares 
      1. The stock price can be gathered from YAHOO Finance 
         1. Most of the time you don’t need preferred equity (can be called preferred stock) but there are some industries which provide this information. This could be in industries such banks or 
      1. P/B → below 1 could mean that the stock is currently undervalued
      1. P/B → roughly around 1 could mean that it is a fair price
      1. P/B → above 1 could mean that the expectation of the market is that more value is being created → most of the time happening in areas of growing and innovation
