## Product Manager (class/ecosystem-level)

### Position Summary
The Product Manager leads the vision, strategy, and execution of a product or platform. This role connects user needs, business objectives, and technical capabilities, ensuring the product delivers meaningful value. The PM works closely with engineering, design, research, and stakeholders to guide the product from concept to launch and continuous improvement.

### Key Responsibilities

#### Product Strategy & Vision
- Define and communicate a clear product vision and roadmap.
- Work with Product Owners and other stakeholders to conduct market, competitor, and user research to identify opportunities.
- Establish success metrics and align product goals with organizational priorities.

#### Requirements & Planning
- Work with RTE, Product Owners, and Scrum Masters to translate user needs into epics, features, and user stories.
- Prioritize the release train backlog using data and stakeholder input.
- Coordinate planning and estimation with engineering and design teams.

#### Execution & Delivery
- Support sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives.
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and product quality standards.
- Monitor product performance and identify areas for improvement.

#### User Insights & Validation
- Work with Product Owners to Gather voice-of-customer insights through interviews, surveys, and analytics.
- Validate solutions through prototypes, usability testing, and experiments.
- Advocate for the customer throughout the product lifecycle.

#### Cross-Functional Collaboration
- Facilitate communication across engineering, design, QA, marketing, and leadership.
- Align stakeholders on goals, progress, and trade-offs.
- Manage risks, dependencies, and timelines.

## Release Train Engineer (class/ecosystem-level)

### Position Summary
The  Engineer (RTE) is a servant leader and coach for the Agile Release Train (ART). The RTE facilitates ART processes, events, and continuous improvement, helping teams deliver value consistently and predictably. This role removes impediments, manages risks, drives program-level execution, and ensures alignment across teams, stakeholders, and leadership.

### Key Responsibilities

#### ART Facilitation & Execution
- Lead and facilitate ART ceremonies including Product Increment (PI) Planning, System Demos, Scrum of Scrums, and Inspect & Adapt.
- Maintain and communicate the ART calendar, milestones, and release schedules.
- Support teams in delivering value aligned with PI objectives.

#### Coaching & Continuous Improvement
- Coach Agile teams, Scrum Masters, and stakeholders on Lean-Agile practices.
- Foster relentless improvement across the ART through retrospectives and data-driven insights.
- Encourage collaboration, transparency, and self-organization.

#### Risk & Dependency Management
- Identify, track, and help resolve cross-team risks, impediments, and dependencies.
- Collaborate with Product Management, System Architects, and leadership to address systemic issues.
- Facilitate risk management using SAFe techniques (e.g., ROAM).

#### Communication & Alignment
- Ensure alignment between business owners, product management, development teams, and leadership.
- Support product strategy execution through coordination of features and capabilities.
- Provide visibility into ART performance through metrics and reporting.

#### Program Support & Governance
- Assist Product Manager with feature readiness, backlog health, and the flow of work across the ART.
- Ensure compliance with organizational governance, quality, and release standards.
- Promote the use of tools and processes that improve ART efficiency and transparency.

#### Metrics & Performance Tracking
- Track and communicate ART flow metrics, predictability, quality indicators, and performance trends.
- Use data to identify bottlenecks, improve flow, and inform planning.
- Support decision-making by providing objective insights into ART progress.

## Systems Architect (class/ecosystem-level)

### Position Summary
The System Architect defines the overall technical vision, architecture, and system design for the product or solution. This role ensures that the architecture supports current business goals, scales for future needs, and aligns with organizational standards. The System Architect works closely with product management, engineering teams, and stakeholders to guide technical decisions, manage technical risk, and ensure cohesive, high-quality system development.

### Key Responsibilities

#### Architectural Vision & Strategy
- Develop and communicate the architectural vision and system roadmap.
- Ensure alignment between business goals, product strategy, and technical design.
- Define architectural standards, patterns, and best practices.

#### System Design & Technical Leadership
- Lead high-level system design, including data flows, integration patterns, and platform architecture.
- Evaluate and select appropriate technologies, tools, and frameworks.
- Provide technical guidance and support to engineering teams during implementation.

#### Governance & Quality Assurance
- Ensure system architecture adheres to security, compliance, performance, and scalability requirements.
- Review designs and code to ensure alignment with architectural standards.
- Establish architectural checkpoints and contribute to release readiness assessments.

#### Collaboration & Cross-Functional Alignment
- Partner with Product Management to ensure features and capabilities have clear architectural support.
- Collaborate with Release Train Engineers, DevOps, UX, and other roles to maintain cohesive system development.
- Communicate architectural decisions and trade-offs with stakeholders at all levels.

#### Risk & Dependency Management
- Identify and mitigate technical risks early in the planning process.
- Manage system-wide dependencies across teams and platforms.
- Ensure long-term maintainability and extensibility of the system.

#### Continuous Improvement & Innovation
- Stay current with emerging technologies and industry trends.
- Recommend innovative solutions to improve system performance and development velocity.
- Drive technical excellence through mentoring, design workshops, and knowledge sharing.

## Product Owner (team-level)

### Position Summary
The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing the value delivered by the development team. This role defines and prioritizes the product backlog, represents customer and stakeholder needs, and ensures the team builds the right features to achieve business outcomes. The Product Owner collaborates closely with the Scrum Master, development team, and stakeholders to refine requirements, clarify priorities, and validate delivered increments. The Product Owner reports to the Product Manager.

### Key Responsibilities

#### Product Vision & Backlog Management
- Work with the Product manager to maintain and communicate a clear product vision and roadmap.
- Own the product backlog, ensuring items are well-defined, prioritized, and ready for development.
- Write user stories with clear acceptance criteria aligned to customer and business needs.

#### Stakeholder Collaboration
- Act as the voice of the customer, gathering and representing stakeholder input.
- Align stakeholders around priorities, trade-offs, and progress toward goals.
- Ensure transparency around backlog status, release plans, and value delivery.

#### Sprint Planning & Execution Support
- Collaborate with the development team to refine backlog items and ensure readiness.
- Participate in sprint planning, reviews, and retrospectives.
- Make timely decisions to unblock development work and ensure smooth sprint execution.

#### Value Delivery & Validation
- Define acceptance criteria and validate completed work against requirements.
- Ensure each increment provides value to users and supports business objectives.
- Track product metrics and user feedback to guide future prioritization.

#### Continuous Improvement
- Analyze user behavior, market trends, and performance metrics to identify improvement opportunities.
- Support experimentation, A/B testing, and feedback loops.
- Drive iterative improvement of both the product and the development process.

## Scrum Master (team-level)

### Position Summary
The Scrum Master is a servant leader who supports the development team and the organization in applying Scrum effectively. This role facilitates Scrum events, removes impediments, fosters collaboration, and promotes continuous improvement. The Scrum Master works closely with the Product Owner and development team to ensure smooth sprint execution, healthy team dynamics, and alignment with Agile principles. The Scrum Master reports to the Release Train Engineer.

### Key Responsibilities

#### Facilitation of Scrum Events
- Plan, schedule, and facilitate Scrum ceremonies including Sprint Planning, Daily Scrum, Sprint Review, and Sprint Retrospective.
- Ensure events are productive, focused, and time-boxed.
- Support the team in maintaining transparency through artifacts such as the Sprint Backlog and Increment.

#### Team Support & Coaching
- Coach the development team on self-organization, accountability, and collaborative problem-solving.
- Help the team understand and apply Agile values and Scrum principles.
- Promote a healthy, psychologically safe team environment.

#### Impediment Removal
- Identify and remove obstacles that block team progress.
- Help the team address inefficiencies and resolve conflicts.
- Escalate issues appropriately when action is needed from outside the team.

#### Collaboration With Product Owner
- Support the Product Owner in maintaining a clear, prioritized backlog.
- Facilitate effective communication between the Product Owner and development team.
- Help ensure user stories are well-understood and ready for development.

#### Process Improvement & Metrics
- Lead continuous improvement efforts based on team feedback and performance data.
- Monitor key metrics such as velocity, predictability, and flow to guide improvements.
- Encourage experimentation and learning to enhance team performance.

- Work with other Scrum Masters to improve Agile practices across teams.

- Help remove systemic impediments affecting multiple teams.
